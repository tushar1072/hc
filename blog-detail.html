How to Open a Restaurant in India:
The Complete 2025 Checklist

May 15, 2025 • Restaurant Setup

Launching a restaurant in India is an exciting venture, but it is also one of the most operationally demanding businesses to start. With complex local tax codes, heavy licensing procedures, and narrow profit margins, having a rigorous, structured roadmap is the difference between thriving and closing down within a year.

In this blueprint, Chef Arjun Mehta outlines the absolute essentials of restaurant setup in India, including licensing, kitchen planning, and recipe design.

"A great menu gets guests through the door once. A great kitchen layout, consistent costing, and strong SOPs make sure the business lasts for years."

Phase 1: Legalities & Licensing

Before purchasing any heavy cooking equipment or signing a lease, ensure you are compliant with municipal laws. You will need to budget both time and money for the following local licensing:

License Name Authority Avg. Processing Time
FSSAI Food License Food Safety & Standards Authority 15 - 30 Days
Health Trade License Local Municipal Corporation 30 - 45 Days
Fire Safety NOC State Fire Department 30 Days
GST Registration Central/State Government 7 - 10 Days
Liquor License (Optional) State Excise Department 3 - 6 Months

Phase 2: Kitchen Design and Ergonomics

The layout of your kitchen directly controls your staff headcount and ticket speed. A poorly mapped prep table or far-removed dishwashing sink results in extra steps, employee fatigue, and food safety delays.

Always ensure your kitchen CAD design features distinct separation between raw receiving, cold preps, high-heat cooking grids, and clean plate pick-ups. This prevents cross-contamination and satisfies FSSAI criteria.

Phase 3: Recipe Standardization and Costing

A common mistake for first-time restaurateurs is creating menus based on gut-feel pricing. Standardize every single dressing, portion weight, and garnish down to the gram. Maintain a target raw ingredient cost below 28-30% of your retail menu listing.
